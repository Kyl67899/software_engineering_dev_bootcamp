FAQs

To maintain an HVAC system, regularly check and replace air filters, clean the coils, inspect the ductwork for leaks, ensure proper insulation, and schedule professional maintenance at least once a year. Additionally, monitor the thermostat settings and keep the outdoor unit clear of debris. Regular maintenance helps.

Regular HVAC maintenance offers several benefits, including improved energy efficiency, extended equipment lifespan, enhanced indoor air quality, reduced repair costs, and consistent comfort levels. It also helps prevent unexpected breakdowns and ensures the system operates safely and effectively.

It is generally recommended to change your HVAC air filter every 1 to 3 months, depending on factors such as the type of filter, the level of dust and allergens in your home, and whether you have pets. Regularly checking the filter and replacing it when it appears dirty can help maintain optimal airflow and indoor air quality.
